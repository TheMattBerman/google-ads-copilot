# benchmarks.md

## Benchmark stance
Benchmarks are context, not truth.

Use them to orient judgment.
Do not let them override:
- query quality
- conversion quality
- business model
- sales cycle
- account maturity

## When benchmarks are useful
- sanity-checking extreme performance claims
- spotting obvious underperformance or overpayment
- setting rough expectations for new accounts

## When benchmarks mislead
- branded traffic contaminates account averages
- tracking is weak
- conversion quality is inconsistent
- niche B2B or unusual sales cycles distort normal ranges
- mixed-intent traffic makes averages look healthier or worse than reality

## Operator rule
If benchmark logic and search-behavior logic disagree, investigate the search behavior first.
