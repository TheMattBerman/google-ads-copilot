# structure-playbook.md

## Core principle
Account structure exists to separate meaning.

A good Google Ads structure does not just organize campaigns neatly.
It creates cleaner optimization buckets so:
- bids learn from similar intent
- RSAs can be more specific
- landing pages can match better
- negatives can route traffic correctly
- performance becomes interpretable

## The structure question
Do the queries in this bucket deserve the same bidding logic, ad language, landing page, and budget treatment?

If not, the structure is probably wrong.

## Common reasons to split
- Brand vs non-brand
- Buyer vs research intent
- Competitor vs generic intent
- Distinct service/product lines
- Geography / market differences
- Match-type control needs

## When NOT to split
Avoid splitting when:
- volume is too low to support learning
- the differences are cosmetic, not meaningful
- the same copy and LP can serve all terms well
- the split adds management cost without control gains

## Structure actions
- Keep
- Clean up
- Split
- Merge
- Route
- Rebuild

## Operator test
Only split if the new structure would meaningfully change:
- bidding
- copy
- landing page
- budget
- reporting decisions
