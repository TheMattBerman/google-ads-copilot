# deliverable-templates.md

## Core rule
Every deliverable should end in decisions.
Not just observations.

Use this general shape unless a task clearly needs a tighter format.

---

## Template — operator summary

### What the account is telling us
2-5 bullets. No fluff.

### What matters most
The 1-3 highest-leverage issues or signals.

### Cut
What traffic, behavior, or structure should be reduced or excluded.

### Isolate
What deserves its own campaign, ad group, or routing rule.

### Scale / support
What looks promising enough to protect, fund, or test harder.

### Do next
1-5 concrete next actions.

### Leave alone
Anything that is still learning or too ambiguous to touch.

### Confidence
High / Medium / Low with one-sentence reason.

### Memory updates
What `workspace/ads/` files were updated.

---

## Template — negative recommendation

For each major negative recommendation:
- Query or cluster
- Why it is bad traffic
- Exclude or isolate?
- Match type recommendation
- Scope recommendation
- Collateral-risk note
- Confidence

---

## Template — structure recommendation

For each structure recommendation:
- What is mixed
- Why it is a problem
- Recommended action: keep / clean up / split / merge / route / rebuild
- What the new bucket should optimize around
- Confidence

---

## Template — tracking diagnosis

### Can we trust the account?
Yes / Mostly / No

### Why
Top 1-3 reasons.

### Risks
- duplicate counting
- bad primary conversion design
- weak value signals
- firing issues
- attribution mismatch

### What decisions should wait
Anything that should not be acted on until tracking is fixed.

### What can still be learned safely
Usually search intent and qualitative query patterns.
