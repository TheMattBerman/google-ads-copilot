# Example: Daily Operator Check

> A SaaS CRM company targeting law firms. The agent pulls live data and tells you what matters today.

## What the account is telling us
- Brand traffic is inflating the perceived strength of non-brand Search.
- A small set of comparison queries is showing stronger commercial intent than the generic bucket.
- Search terms with "jobs" and "what is" modifiers are leaking spend.

## What matters most
1. Non-brand reporting is muddy because branded demand is bleeding into it.
2. Comparison intent is promising enough to isolate.
3. Wasted educational/job-seeker traffic is cheap but still distorting optimization.

## Cut
- Add negatives for the employment cluster.
- Separate or exclude broad educational modifiers from the BOF bucket.

## Isolate
- Move `alternatives`, `vs`, and `pricing` style terms into their own comparison-focused structure.

## Scale / support
- Protect the small set of high-intent demo/pricing queries.
- Support comparison traffic with more specific copy instead of generic ads.

## Do next
1. Add phrase negatives for jobs/careers cluster.
2. Split brand from non-brand cleanly.
3. Create a comparison ad group or campaign.

## Leave alone
- One low-volume ad group is too thin to judge yet. Let it gather more data before restructuring.

## Confidence
Medium — enough query evidence to act, but conversion tracking quality should still be verified.
