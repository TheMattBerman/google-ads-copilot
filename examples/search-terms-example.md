# Example: Search Terms Analysis

> Where the waste shows up. The agent reads every search term, clusters by intent, and tells you what to cut, what to isolate, and what to scale.

## What the account is telling us
- The generic campaign is buying both high-intent software evaluation traffic and low-intent educational traffic.
- A few comparison-style modifiers are outperforming the rest of the bucket.
- Job-seeker and support-style queries are easy exclusion wins.

## What matters most
1. Query quality is mixed enough that averages are hiding what is actually working.
2. Some waste should be excluded.
3. Some "waste" is really a structure problem and deserves isolation instead.

## Cut
- `jobs`, `careers`, `login`, `support`
- likely educational modifiers for BOF campaign: `what is`, `guide`, `tutorial`

## Isolate
- `alternatives`, `vs`, `pricing`, `demo` style searches

## Scale / support
- buyer-language modifiers around `demo`, `pricing`, and vertical-specific queries

## Negative recommendations
- `jobs` — phrase — shared list — low collateral risk
- `login` — phrase — campaign/shared — low collateral risk
- `what is` — phrase — campaign-level for BOF bucket — medium collateral risk if content-led acquisition exists

## Messaging clues
- "law firm crm"
- "demo"
- "pricing"
- "alternatives"
