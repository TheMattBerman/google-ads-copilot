# Example: Intent Map

> The core analytical artifact. This is what most accounts have never seen — their search queries classified by what people actually *meant*, not just what they typed.

## What the account is telling us
The account is buying three very different realities at once: true buyer intent, lightweight educational curiosity, and competitor/comparison traffic. Right now they're too blended to optimize cleanly.

## Intent Map
### Proven Buyer Intent
- `crm demo for law firms`
- `legal crm pricing`
- `best crm for small law firm`

### Mixed / Ambiguous Intent
- `crm for lawyers`
- `law firm client management software`

### Waste / Exclusion Signals
- `what is crm`
- `crm jobs`
- `free crm template`

### Competitor / Comparison Intent
- `hubspot alternatives for law firms`
- `clio vs hubspot crm`

### Branded Intent
- branded product and founder searches

## Structural implications
- Branded terms need their own protected bucket.
- Comparison traffic deserves dedicated copy and LP treatment.
- Educational and job-seeker traffic should not share the same optimization bucket as demo/pricing searches.

## Next actions
- Cut: employment + obvious research junk
- Isolate: comparison terms
- Protect: branded + pricing/demo terms
- Test: pain-led copy for the mixed-intent cluster
